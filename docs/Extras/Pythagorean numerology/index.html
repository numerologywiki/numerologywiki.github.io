Pythagorean numerology

Pythagorean numerology is a Western system of numerology based on the ideas of the ancient Greek philosopher and mathematician Pythagoras, who taught that numbers are the fundamental building blocks of reality and carry spiritual or “vibrational” meaning. It is also commonly called modern or Western numerology and is one of the most widely used numerology systems today.[1][3][4][6][7]

How it works​

In the Pythagorean system, each letter of the modern Latin alphabet is assigned a number from 1 to 9 (A=1, B=2, … I=9, J=1, K=2, etc.), and names or words are converted into numbers by adding these values and then reducing them to a single digit (or keeping certain “master numbers” like 11, 22, 33). These resulting numbers are interpreted as revealing traits, life purpose, challenges, and tendencies.[2][3][5][6]

Core numbers in practice​

Typical readings compute several key numbers from your birth date and full name, such as:

  • Life Path Number (from your birth date), said to reflect your overall life purpose and direction.[3][6][2]
  • Expression/Name Number, Soul Urge Number, and Personality Number, each derived from different parts of your name and interpreted as aspects of your character and how others see you.[4][2][3]

Underlying philosophy​

Pythagorean numerology rests on the belief that numbers are more than mathematical tools—they express hidden patterns and energies that shape human experience. Practitioners use the system as a kind of symbolic language to gain self‑insight, rather than as strict scientific prediction.[6][7][3][4]
